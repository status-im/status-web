20:T2fcf,<p>A <strong>stablecoin</strong> is a crypto token pegged to a stable asset, usually the US dollar, to reduce price swings. Stablecoins bridge traditional finance and blockchain. They power payments, savings, and collateral without crypto volatility. Three main types exist: collateral-backed, algorithmic, and yield-bearing. Each uses a different method to hold a $1.00 peg.</p><h2 id="what-is-a-stablecoin">What Is a Stablecoin?</h2><p>Stablecoins solve one core problem: price volatility. Bitcoin and Ethereum prices can swing wildly each day. That makes them poor choices for commerce, lending, or savings.</p><p>A stablecoin aims to hold a $1.00 target price. It does this through reserves or supply controls.</p><p>Stablecoins fill four main roles:</p><ul><li>Payment rails inside blockchain networks</li><li>Collateral for lending protocols</li><li>Settlement layers for decentralized exchanges</li><li>Stores of value in volatile markets</li></ul><p>Without stablecoins, DeFi users would need to convert to fiat constantly. Stablecoins remove that friction.</p><h2 id="backed-collateral-backed-stablecoins">Backed (Collateral-Backed) Stablecoins</h2><p><strong>Backed stablecoins</strong> use collateral to hold their peg. That collateral can be fiat currency, crypto assets, or physical commodities.</p><h3 id="fiat-backed-model">Fiat-Backed Model</h3><p>Issuers hold $1 USD in reserve for every token in circulation. This is the simplest and most common model.</p><p>Examples: <strong>USDC</strong> (Circle), <strong>USDT</strong> (Tether).</p><p>How it works:</p><ol><li>A user deposits $1,000 USD with the issuer.</li><li>The issuer mints 1,000 stablecoin tokens.</li><li>The user can redeem tokens back to fiat at a 1:1 rate.</li></ol>

<h2 id="yield-bearing-stablecoins">Yield-Bearing Stablecoins</h2><p><strong>Yield-bearing stablecoins</strong> hold a $1 peg while earning returns for their holders. Users gain passive income without managing protocols themselves.</p><p>Issuers deposit collateral into external yield sources like lending markets. The protocol sends earned yield back to holders. The peg stays at $1, but the holder's balance grows over time.</p><h3 id="example-sdai">Example: sDAI</h3><p><strong>sDAI</strong> (Savings DAI) is issued by MakerDAO. It deposits DAI into Sky Protocol. Yield flows back to holders on its own, with no manual action required.</p><h3 id="example-gusd">Example: GUSD</h3><p><a href="https://docs.status.network/tokenomics/pre-deposits"><strong>GUSD</strong></a> is a yield-bearing meta-stablecoin on <a href="https://status.network/"><strong>Status Network</strong></a>. It serves as the network's primary savings tool.</p>

Use USDC.</li><li>Looking for decentralized borrowing? Use Dai.</li><li>Want to earn yield on your savings? Use sDAI or <strong>GUSD</strong>.</li></ul><p>Yield-bearing stablecoins will likely grow as markets mature.
