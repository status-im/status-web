21:T6859,<p><a href="https://status.network/">Status Network</a> is a fully gasless Ethereum Layer 2 built on reputation, composable privacy, and network-level spam protection. Designed for both humans and bots, it scales Ethereum using <a href="https://linea.build/">Linea’s</a> open-source ZK-EVM stack. Unlike traditional L2s that depend on gas fees as their primary revenue source, Status Network funds itself through productive capital on Layer 1 and fees from native apps on Layer 2, allowing active users to transact for free.</p><p>The economic model has two reinforcing revenue sources: yield generated by bridged assets put to work on Ethereum mainnet, and fees from native applications including a DEX, CDP stablecoin, token launchpad, and privacy layer. All revenue flows into a community-governed <a href="https://docs.status.network/tokenomics/public-funding">Apps Funding Pool</a> allocated by <a href="https://docs.status.network/tokenomics/karmic-tokenomics">Karma</a> holders, the network’s soulbound reputation token. Zero gas is not a subsidy. It is the end result of replacing fee-based revenue with yield-backed economics.</p><h2 id="why-zero-gas-requires-a-different-economic-model">Why Zero Gas Requires a Different Economic Model</h2><p>A blockchain economic model defines how a network pays for execution, security, and development. Most Layer 2 solutions rely on gas fees charged per transaction. This creates friction for social apps, games, and everyday use cases where per-action costs discourage organic activity and compromise privacy, gas top-ups link accounts, gas prices fingerprint wallets, and funding trails correlate identities across transactions.</p><p>Status Network removes gas fees entirely at the protocol level. Without gas as a revenue source, the network needs an alternative economic model. The answer is productive capital: assets bridged to the network are put to work on Layer 1 to generate yield, and that yield, alongside native app fees, funds everything.</p><p>This is not gas abstraction through paymasters. Paymasters still leak metadata. Status Network removes the source of leakage entirely.</p><h2 id="revenue-sources-how-status-network-funds-itself">Revenue Sources: How Status Network Funds Itself</h2><p>Status Network derives revenue from four sources. Together they replace sequencer fee revenue and create what the network calls “dual upside”: income that grows with both TVL and transaction volume.</p><h3 id="1-native-yield-from-bridged-capital-on-layer-1">1. Native Yield from Bridged Capital on Layer 1</h3><p>When users bridge assets to Status Network, those assets are not held idle. They are deployed into yield-generating strategies on Ethereum mainnet:</p><ul><li><strong>ETH</strong> is deposited into the L1 bridge contract and staked through a <a href="https://lido.fi/">Lido</a> V3 stVault. This architecture was co-designed by Status Network and implemented by the Linea and Lido teams. No stETH is minted for users, ETH bridges cleanly to L2, and staking yield is bridged separately to the funding pool.</li><li><strong>Stablecoins</strong> (USDC, USDT, USDS) are deployed into <a href="https://morpho.org/">Morpho</a> lending and Sky savings strategies via Generic Protocol, arriving on L2 as GUSD. Yield bridges separately to the funding pool.</li></ul><p>Users interact with clean, simple assets on L2 (ETH and GUSD). Behind the scenes, all yield generated on L1 is bridged into the shared funding pool on L2.</p><h3 id="2-native-dex-swap-fees">2. Native DEX Swap Fees</h3><p><a href="https://www.orvex.fi/">Orvex</a>, the native decentralized exchange on Status Network, charges a small fee on trades. A portion flows into the funding pool. Liquidity providers also earn extra incentives from native yield on top of standard LP fees, creating an additional pull for deep liquidity.</p><h3 id="3-premium-gas-fees">3. Premium Gas Fees</h3><p>Users exceeding their free transaction quota, determined by their Karma tier, pay a premium gas fee. This fee is split between Status Network operations and the RLN (Rate Limiting Nullifier) spam prevention mechanism. Paying the premium also earns the user additional Karma, which can unlock higher free transaction limits over time.</p><h3 id="4-future-native-app-fees">4. Future Native App Fees</h3><p>Additional revenue streams are planned from native apps launching on the network:</p><ul><li><strong>FIRM</strong>: a decentralised CDP stablecoin (Liquity V2 fork), mint USF against ETH and core assets</li><li><strong>Punk.fun</strong>: permissionless token launchpad with fair price discovery</li><li><strong>Bermuda</strong>: composable privacy layer enabling private balances, transactions, and DeFi interactions across all native apps</li><li><strong>Lending markets</strong>: borrowing using core assets as collateral</li></ul><p>Each native app contributes a share of generated fees back into the funding pool, expanding the revenue base as the ecosystem grows.</p><h2 id="the-apps-funding-pool-how-revenue-is-allocated">The Apps Funding Pool: How Revenue Is Allocated</h2><p>All revenue, L1 yield and L2 native app fees, flows into the Apps Funding Pool, a shared community-governed treasury. This is the core economic mechanism that replaces developer grants, external fundraising, and token emissions as the primary source of builder capital on Status Network.</p><p>The funding pool is governed by Karma holders. Karma is a soulbound non-transferable reputation token, it cannot be bought or transferred, only earned through contribution. This means allocation power over the network’s yield stays with the people who actually build and use it, not those who simply buy tokens.</p><p>Karma holders vote on:</p><ul><li>Developer grants and app funding</li><li>Liquidity incentives for DEX pairs and curated pools</li><li>Infrastructure bounties</li><li>Network economic and operational parameters</li></ul><p>Initially, funding recipients will be limited to maintain quality control. As the pool grows, more projects will be supported. Developers are funded by network activity, not external investors.</p><h2 id="karma-reputation-as-the-access-primitive">Karma: Reputation as the Access Primitive</h2><p>Karma is the unified reputation primitive of Status Network. It is a soulbound governance token that determines free transaction throughput, spam resistance, governance power over the funding pool, and access to private gasless accounts. It cannot be bought, sold, or transferred. No entity, including the Status team, has any advantage in acquiring it.</p><p>Karma is earned through six mechanisms:</p><ol><li><a href="https://hub.status.network/stake" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Staking SNT</strong></a>, 35% of weekly Karma minting at launch. The longer SNT is staked or locked, the higher the earning rate (up to 4x multiplier over 4 years without lock, or 9x with a 4-year lock).</li><li><strong>Providing liquidity and using apps</strong>, 60% of weekly Karma minting. Both developers and active users earn Karma through transaction volume and app traction.</li><li><strong>Sequencer tips and direct donations</strong>, 5% of weekly Karma minting. Contributing directly to the public funding pool earns Karma.</li></ol><p>Karma minting allocations are set at launch and may evolve as the network matures.</p><p>Karma grants access to:</p><ul><li><strong>Zero gas transaction quotas</strong>: tiered system, anywhere from a few free transactions per day up to hundreds of thousands, scaled by Karma tier</li><li><strong>Governance over the funding pool</strong>: Karma holders vote on yield allocation, liquidity incentives, and network parameters</li><li><strong>Private gasless accounts</strong>: Karma unlocks access to ephemeral stealth accounts and relayers that decouple activity from identity</li><li><strong>Free cover traffic</strong>: Karma holders can generate decoy transactions through the privacy layer at zero cost, amplifying anonymity sets</li><li><strong>Reputation and perks</strong>: high Karma signals power users; apps have strong incentives to attract and reward high-Karma participants</li></ul><p>Governance power is earned, not purchased. This prevents plutocratic capture and keeps decision-making aligned with network contributors.</p><h2 id="spam-protection-how-zero-gas-stays-abuse-free">Spam Protection: How Zero Gas Stays Abuse-Free</h2><p>Zero gas requires a non-financial mechanism for rate limiting. Status Network uses <a href="https://docs.status.network/general-info/gasless-transactions">Rate Limiting Nullifiers</a> (RLN), a zero-knowledge protocol developed by Logos and already deployed in Logos Messaging, Railgun, and The Graph’s indexer coordination.</p><p>How RLN works on Status Network:</p><ul><li>Users generate zero-knowledge proofs of their RLN group membership, which determines their free transaction throughput tier based on Karma.</li><li>Each transaction includes a unique nullifier derived from the user’s secret key. Exceeding the quota within an epoch makes the secret key recoverable, exposing the user.</li><li>Users who exceed limits are added to a temporary Deny List. Paying a premium gas fee removes them and earns additional Karma.</li><li>RLN uses Sparse Merkle Trees (height 20, supporting 1M accounts) and Shamir’s Secret Sharing. Proofs are cryptographic, not account-based, users cannot be deanonymised by transaction patterns.</li></ul><p>The same RLN primitive has been proposed by Buterin and Crapis for private, anonymous API metering including LLM inference and RPC endpoints. This positions Status Network’s spam protection as forward-compatible infrastructure for the intersection of crypto and AI.</p><h2 id="the-economic-flywheel">The Economic Flywheel</h2><p>The economic model is self-reinforcing. More capital and activity feed a larger funding pool, which funds better incentives, which attract more capital and activity:</p><ol><li>Users bridge ETH and stablecoins, earning Karma and increasing TVL.</li><li>Bridged capital generates yield on L1 via Lido staking and stablecoin strategies.</li><li>Native app fees accumulate alongside L1 yield in the funding pool.</li><li>Karma holders vote to allocate the pool to liquidity providers and app builders.</li><li>Funded apps onboard more users, increasing DEX volume and native app fee revenue.</li><li>Higher TVL and volume attract more capital, closing the loop.</li></ol><p>Because Karma is earned through contribution, governance power stays aligned with the people who grow the network. The flywheel is self-reinforcing: more capital and activity feed a bigger pool, which funds better incentives, which attract more capital and activity.</p><h2 id="building-on-status-network-a-developer-example">Building on Status Network: A Developer Example</h2><p>A developer launching a social app on Status Network does not need to:</p><ul><li>Pay gas for user transactions, Karma holders transact for free</li><li>Compete for expensive block space</li><li>Rely on token emissions to subsidise user activity</li><li>Build custom gas management infrastructure for bots or automations</li></ul><p>Instead:</p><ul><li>Active users earn Karma through app interactions</li><li>High app usage increases the developer’s Karma voting weight</li><li>The developer applies for grants from the Apps Funding Pool, funded by L1 yield</li><li>Community Karma voting allocates sustained capital to successful apps</li></ul><p>Developer grants depend on real users and genuine traction.
