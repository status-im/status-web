28:T27c5,<p>A <strong>liquidity pool</strong> is a smart contract holding paired tokens that traders swap against instead of matching orders with a counterparty. An <strong>automated market maker</strong> (AMM) prices each trade using a math formula, removing the need for order books. On <a href="https://status.network/"><strong>Status Network</strong></a>, the native DEX <a href="https://status.network/blog/orvex-dex-zero-fee-trading"><strong>Orvex</strong> </a>runs this AMM model with zero gas fees, plus extra yield from productive Layer 1 capital.</p><h2 id="what-is-a-liquidity-pool">What Is a Liquidity Pool?</h2><p>Traditional exchanges use order books. Buyers post bids. Sellers post asks. A trade happens when prices match.</p><p>Liquidity pools skip this entirely.</p><p>A pool is a smart contract holding two tokens. Think ETH and a stablecoin. Anyone can deposit both tokens in equal value. Those depositors are called <strong>liquidity providers</strong> (LPs).</p><p>When a trader wants to swap Token A for Token B, they send Token A into the pool. The pool sends Token B back. No counterparty needed. No waiting for a match.</p><p>The pool charges a small fee on each swap. That fee goes to the LPs as a reward.</p><h2 id="how-does-an-automated-market-maker-work">How Does an Automated Market Maker Work?</h2><p>An <strong>AMM</strong> is the pricing engine behind every liquidity pool. The most common formula is the constant product equation:</p><p><strong>x × y = k</strong></p><p>Here, x is the quantity of Token A. y is the quantity of Token B. k is a constant.</p><p>When someone buys Token A, x decreases. To keep k constant, y must increase. This pushes Token A's price up. The formula self-adjusts with every trade.</p><p>No one sets the price. Supply and demand inside the pool do.</p><h3 id="why-amms-replaced-order-books-in-defi">Why AMMs Replaced Order Books in DeFi</h3><p>Order books need market makers. They need high throughput and low latency. That works on centralized exchanges.</p><p>On a blockchain, posting and canceling orders costs gas. Slow block times create stale quotes. Thin order books lead to bad prices.</p><p>AMMs solve all three problems:</p><ul><li>Pricing is automatic and instant</li><li>No orders to post or cancel</li><li>Any token pair can have a pool from day one</li></ul><p>This is why nearly every decentralized exchange uses the AMM model.</p><h2 id="what-do-liquidity-providers-earn">What Do Liquidity Providers Earn?</h2><p>LPs earn a share of swap fees proportional to their deposit. If you provide 1% of a pool, you earn 1% of all fees that pool collects.</p><p>Some protocols add token incentives on top. These "farming" rewards attract deeper liquidity, which means better prices for traders.</p><h3 id="the-tradeoff-impermanent-loss">The Tradeoff: Impermanent Loss</h3><p>LP returns are not risk-free. When token prices diverge, LPs may end up with less value than if they simply held both tokens.</p><p>This is called <strong>impermanent loss</strong>. It becomes permanent only if you withdraw while prices are still diverged.</p><p>Pools with correlated assets (like two stablecoins) carry lower impermanent loss risk. Pools with volatile pairs carry more.</p><h2 id="how-liquidity-pools-work-on-layer-2">How Liquidity Pools Work on Layer 2</h2><p>Ethereum mainnet (Layer 1) handles roughly 15 transactions per second. Gas fees spike during high demand. Both problems hit DEX traders hard.</p><p><strong>Layer 2</strong> networks process transactions off the main chain and post proofs back to Ethereum. This delivers higher throughput and lower costs.</p><p>For AMMs, Layer 2 means:</p><ul><li>Faster swap confirmations</li><li>Lower (or zero) transaction costs</li><li>More frequent arbitrage, which keeps pool prices accurate</li></ul><p>The result: tighter spreads and better execution for traders. More fee volume for LPs.</p><h2 id="how-orvex-dex-uses-liquidity-pools-on-status-network">How Orvex DEX Uses Liquidity Pools on Status Network</h2><p><strong>Orvex</strong> is the native DEX on <strong>Status Network</strong>, an Ethereum Layer 2 built on the <a href="https://status.app/blog/put-your-linea-tokens-to-work-ahead-of-status-network-launch"><strong>Linea</strong></a> zkEVM stack. It uses the AMM model described above, with key differences tied to the network's gasless architecture.</p><h3 id="zero-gas-fees-for-swaps">Zero Gas Fees for Swaps</h3><p>On most DEXs, every swap costs gas. On Status Network, transactions are gasless. The network funds execution through its <a href="https://status.network/blog/yield-farming-vs-native-yield"><strong>native yield</strong></a><strong> engine</strong>, not through user fees.</p><p>Bridged ETH converts to stETH via Lido. Bridged stablecoins generate yield through lending protocols like Morpho. A 30% share of that yield funds network operations.</p><p>Traders on <strong>Orvex</strong> pay zero gas. Swap fees still apply (those go to LPs), but the execution cost disappears.</p><h3 id="native-yield-on-top-of-swap-fees">Native Yield on Top of Swap Fees</h3><p>LPs on Orvex earn standard swap fees. They also earn a share of extra incentives funded by the network's native yield and fee pool.</p><p>This stacks two revenue streams:</p><ul><li>Swap fees from trading activity</li><li>Native yield incentives allocated by <a href="https://status.network/blog/what-is-a-stablecoin-backed-algorithmic-and-yield-bearing-types"><strong>Karma</strong></a> holders</li></ul><h3 id="karma-governed-incentive-gauges">Karma-Governed Incentive Gauges</h3><p><strong>Karma</strong> is a soulbound reputation token <a href="https://docs.status.network/">on Status Network</a>. It cannot be bought, sold, or transferred. Users earn it by staking <strong>SNT</strong>, bridging assets, providing liquidity, or building apps.</p><p>Karma holders vote on which Orvex liquidity pools receive extra incentives. This is the gauge system.</p><p>Pools that attract Karma votes receive more rewards. This directs liquidity to the pairs the community values most.</p><h3 id="how-this-connects-to-the-economic-flywheel">How This Connects to the Economic Flywheel</h3><p>The cycle works like this:</p><ol><li>Users bridge ETH or stablecoins to Status Network</li><li>Those assets generate Layer 1 yield automatically</li><li>Yield and Orvex swap fees flow into the <a href="https://status.network/blog/status-network-economic-model-yield-funding-pool-and-zero-gas">funding pool</a></li><li>Karma holders direct incentives to Orvex pools and app builders</li><li>Better incentives attract more LPs and more trading volume</li><li>Higher volume generates more fees, restarting the cycle</li></ol><p>This is the core economic model: capital coordination replaces gas extraction.</p><h2 id="liquidity-pools-vs-order-books-quick-comparison">Liquidity Pools vs. Order Books: Quick Comparison</h2>
