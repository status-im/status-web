Because Karma is soulbound, governance influence must be earned through sustained participation, it cannot be purchased on secondary markets.</p><h3 id="access-to-private-gasless-infrastructure">Access to Private Gasless Infrastructure</h3><p>Karma unlocks access to private gasless ephemeral accounts and relayers. These enable confidential strategies, stealth accounts with no funding fingerprints, and cover traffic generation, decoy transactions that amplify anonymity sets at zero cost within your tier quota. Without gas fees, there are no funding trails linking accounts, no gas price fingerprinting, no timing correlations to exploit.</p><h2 id="spam-protection-and-slashing">Spam Protection and Slashing</h2><p>The throughput tier system has two enforcement layers:</p><p><strong>Soft layer (tier violations):</strong> When you exceed your tier quota, you're placed on a temporary deny list. Gasless access is suspended until the reset interval expires. No Karma is lost. You can still transact by paying premium gas.</p><p><strong>Hard layer (RLN violations):</strong> The Rate Limiting Nullifier sets a global maximum transaction rate. If you exceed it, the RLN construction exposes your secret through Shamir's Secret Sharing, two proofs using the same nullifier within the same epoch are sufficient for any observer to recover the underlying key. This triggers slashing: 100% of your total Karma balance is burned, with 50% minted as a reward to the slasher who detected the violation and 50% permanently burned. Your RLN membership is revoked. Recovery requires re-earning sufficient Karma through legitimate participation.</p><p>The 100% slash is justified by the two-layer design. By the time RLN slashing triggers, two distinct thresholds have already been violated. Accidental over-usage is caught by the soft layer with no penalty. The hard layer has strong evidence of deliberate abuse.</p><p>Because Karma is earned through time and contribution, slashing carries real opportunity cost. You cannot buy back a slashed balance. You re-earn it.</p><h2 id="the-karmic-flywheel">The Karmic Flywheel</h2><p>SNT staking feeds directly into the core economic loop:</p><ol><li>Capital bridges into the network (ETH → staked via Lido stVaults, stablecoins → deployed into Morpho/Sky)</li><li>L1 yield accumulates and bridges into the shared funding pool on L2</li><li>Native apps (Orvex, FIRM, Punk.fun, Bermuda) generate fees into the same pool</li><li>Karma holders vote on how the pool is allocated, liquidity incentives, app builder grants, operations</li><li>Better incentives attract more users and capital</li><li>More TVL generates more yield. More activity generates more fees.</li><li>Back to step 1</li></ol><p>SNT stakers hold governance weight from day one. The 35% SNT staking bucket is a deliberate bootstrap weight: the network needs a strong initial base of staked SNT to power RLN-based spam protection. As TVL deepens and onchain activity grows, governance can vote to rebalance the ratio, shifting more emission toward liquidity and app usage where marginal Karma has higher network growth impact.</p><h2 id="genesis-allocation-early-karma-weight">Genesis Allocation: Early Karma Weight</h2><p>Pre-deposit participants received genesis Karma proportional to deposit size and time in the vault. With approximately 1,400 pre-depositors at launch, average allocation is ~535,000 Karma per depositor, placing the average early participant at the High-Throughput tier (108,000 tx/day). Large depositors reach S-Tier or Legendary. This is the intended reward for committing capital ahead of mainnet.</p><p>After year one, the 2B total cumulative supply is split roughly 50/50 between pre-deposit genesis holders and year-one earners. Within the earner pool: SNT stakers hold 350M, LPs and users hold 600M, operations contributors hold 50M. No single category dominates. The soulbound constraint prevents accumulation through acquisition.</p><h2 id="getting-started-with-snt-staking">Getting Started with SNT Staking</h2><ol><li><strong>Obtain SNT </strong>- Purchase on decentralized exchanges or transfer from existing SNT holdings</li><li><strong>Access Status Network </strong>- Add the network to your wallet via the <a href="https://docs.status.network/">official docs</a></li><li><strong>Stake via the contract </strong>- Approve SNT and call the staking contract (address published on docs at launch)</li><li><strong>Monitor Karma accrual </strong>- Virtual Karma accrues continuously; check your account dashboard</li><li><strong>Redeem before voting </strong>- Convert virtual Karma to actual Karma to unlock governance voting power</li></ol><p>Pre-deposits are live at <a href="https://hub.status.network/pre-deposits">hub.status.network/pre-deposits</a>.</p><h2 id="frequently-asked-questions">Frequently Asked Questions</h2><p><strong>Can I unstake SNT at any time?</strong></p><p>Yes. If you restake, you start accumulating multiplier points from scratch.</p><p><strong>Does SNT staking require gas fees?</strong></p><p>The initial staking transaction costs gas on Ethereum or Status Network depending on where you execute. Once you've earned sufficient Karma, subsequent Status Network transactions become gasless up to your tier quota.</p><p><strong>What happens to Karma if I unstake?</strong></p><p>Unstaking does not burn your existing Karma balance. Your accumulated Karma stays with your address. Your multiplier resets, so the future accrual rate drops, but historical Karma, and the governance and throughput access it unlocks, is yours.</p><p><strong>Can I transfer Karma to another wallet?</strong></p><p>No. Karma is soulbound, permanently tied to your address. Transfers, approvals, and allowances are disabled. This ensures Karma represents genuine contribution, not wealth.</p><p><strong>Is there a minimum SNT amount to stake?</strong></p><p>The minimum will be specified in the staking contract documentation.
