28:T2517,<p><a href="https://status.app/blog/status-network-opens-the-gusd-vault-final-phase-before-mainnet">GUSD</a> is a yield-bearing meta-stablecoin built for Status Network. Deposit USDC, USDT, or USDS and receive GUSD, a single token that automatically allocates your capital across diversified lending protocols and compounds the returns. Thirty percent of that yield funds Status Network operations. The rest goes to you.</p><p>That is the model: users earn yield, the network sustains itself, and nobody pays gas fees to make it work.</p><h2 id="the-problem-gusd-solves">The Problem GUSD Solves</h2><p>Stablecoins are inert by default. Held in a wallet, USDC preserves value but generates nothing. To earn yield, users have to actively deposit into lending protocols like Morpho or Sky, and once they do, they are exposed to a single strategy, a single risk surface, and the ongoing overhead of managing it.</p><p>GUSD removes that tradeoff. It bundles multiple yield strategies into one composable token, allocating deposits across vetted lending protocols automatically. Users get diversification without active management, and yield without giving up a composable asset they can use elsewhere in the ecosystem.</p><p>This is what "meta-stablecoin" means: GUSD exists one layer above traditional stablecoins, packaging their yield potential into something simpler to hold.</p><h2 id="how-gusd-works">How GUSD Works</h2><h3 id="minting-and-deposits">Minting and Deposits</h3><p>Deposit USDC, USDT, or USDS into the GUSD vault and receive GUSD at a 1:1 ratio. Your stablecoins are pooled with other deposits and deployed across yield-generating protocols, initially through Generic Protocol, which routes capital to Morpho and Sky (formerly MakerDAO's Dai Savings Rate).</p><h3 id="how-yield-accrues">How Yield Accrues</h3><p>GUSD tokens do not multiply over time. Instead, the redemption value per token increases as the vault's underlying assets earn yield. Hold 1,000 GUSD while the vault grows 5% and your 1,000 tokens redeem for 5% more stablecoins on withdrawal. The mechanism is identical to how Lido's stETH or MakerDAO's sDAI work.</p><h3 id="fee-split-and-network-funding">Fee Split and Network Funding</h3><p>Of the total yield generated by the vault, 30% flows into Status Network's Apps Funding Pool, the on-chain treasury that Karma holders vote to allocate toward developers and liquidity providers. The remaining 70% compounds for GUSD holders.</p><p>Holding GUSD is therefore a dual action: you earn yield and you fund the network that makes gasless transactions possible.</p><h2 id="gusds-role-in-the-status-network-flywheel">GUSD's Role in the Status Network Flywheel</h2><p>Status Network is a gasless Ethereum L2. It does not charge sequencer fees or extract value through gas. Instead, it funds itself through the productive return on bridged capital. GUSD is the user-facing engine of that model.</p><p>The flywheel works like this:</p><ol><li>Users deposit stablecoins into GUSD and bridge to Status Network.</li><li>The vault generates yield on Morpho and Sky.</li><li>30% of that yield enters the Apps Funding Pool.</li><li>Karma holders vote to distribute funding to builders and liquidity providers.</li><li>A healthier ecosystem attracts more users and more TVL.</li><li>More TVL generates more yield, funding more development.</li></ol><p>No gas extraction. No token inflation. No short-term incentive programs that dilute holders. The network is funded by capital that would otherwise be sitting idle.</p><h2 id="gusd-vs-other-yield-bearing-stablecoins">GUSD vs. Other Yield-Bearing Stablecoins</h2><h3 id="gusd-vs-sdai">GUSD vs. sDAI</h3><p>sDAI earns yield from the Dai Savings Rate, funded by MakerDAO's stability fees. It accepts only DAI and allocates to a single strategy.</p><p>GUSD accepts USDC, USDT, and USDS, allocates across multiple lending protocols, and routes a portion of yield toward a specific L2 ecosystem. For users who want diversification and a direct stake in network sustainability, GUSD goes further.</p><h3 id="gusd-vs-depositing-directly-into-morpho">GUSD vs. Depositing Directly into Morpho</h3><p>Direct Morpho deposits give users full control over which lending markets they enter. That control comes with overhead: choosing markets, monitoring positions, rebalancing manually.</p><p>GUSD abstracts all of that. It routes capital to the best-yielding markets automatically and requires no active management. For users who want Morpho-level yield without Morpho-level involvement, GUSD is the simpler path.</p><h3 id="gusd-vs-usdcusdt">GUSD vs. USDC/USDT</h3><p>USDC and USDT are reserve-backed stablecoins. They maintain a 1:1 peg through issuer guarantees and are designed for stability and liquidity, not yield.</p><p>GUSD is a yield-bearing wrapper, not a reserve-backed asset. Its redemption value grows over time rather than staying fixed, and its primary purpose is compounding returns rather than serving as a medium of exchange. These are different instruments for different purposes.</p><h2 id="risks-to-understand-before-depositing">Risks to Understand Before Depositing</h2><p><strong>Smart contract risk.</strong> The GUSD vault must be audited before depositing significant capital. Vault bugs can result in fund loss. Always verify current audit status through official Status Network documentation or the vault's GitHub repository.</p><p><strong>Underlying protocol risk.</strong> When GUSD allocates to Morpho or Sky, holders inherit the risk profile of those protocols. A failure in an underlying market, whether from a code exploit or undercollateralized lending, could result in losses. Diversification across protocols reduces concentration risk, but does not eliminate it.</p><p><strong>Yield variability.</strong> Stablecoin lending yields fluctuate with supply, demand, and broader market conditions. GUSD's returns are not fixed and can compress significantly during low-activity periods.</p><p><strong>Redemption liquidity.</strong> Depending on vault configuration, withdrawals may be subject to delays or liquidity constraints. Check current documentation before committing capital you may need on short notice.</p><h2 id="how-to-get-started-with-gusd">How to Get Started with GUSD</h2><ol><li>Hold USDC, USDT, or USDS on Ethereum mainnet.</li><li>Deposit into the GUSD vault via the Status Network interface or a supported partner app.</li><li>Receive GUSD at a 1:1 initial ratio.</li><li>Bridge GUSD to Status Network or hold on Ethereum.</li><li>Yield compounds automatically, no further action required.</li><li>Withdraw at any time by returning GUSD to the vault.</li></ol><p>The vault is non-custodial. You retain control of your private keys throughout.</p><h2 id="gusd-within-the-broader-status-ecosystem">GUSD Within the Broader Status Ecosystem</h2><p>GUSD is one of several native applications built on Status Network:</p><ul><li><strong>Orvex</strong> is the native DEX for trading and liquidity provision.</li><li><strong>FIRM</strong> is a CDP-based stablecoin issuer that mints USF against collateral.</li><li><strong>Bermuda</strong> and other ecosystem apps generate fees that flow into the same Apps Funding Pool.</li></ul><p>Each application contributes revenue to the network. GUSD's contribution is particularly significant because stablecoin yield is predictable and scales linearly with TVL, making it the most reliable funding source as the network grows.</p><h2 id="frequently-asked-questions">Frequently Asked Questions</h2><p><strong>What is the difference between GUSD and USDC?</strong></p><p>USDC is a reserve-backed stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the dollar through issuer guarantees. GUSD is a yield-bearing wrapper that deploys stablecoins into lending protocols and compounds the returns. USDC is for stability and liquidity. GUSD is for yield.</p><p><strong>Can I trade GUSD on Status Network?</strong></p><p>Yes. GUSD can be traded on Orvex, the native DEX on Status Network, or bridged back to Ethereum for use on other platforms.</p><p><strong>What happens if an underlying lending protocol fails?</strong></p><p>If Morpho or Sky experiences a failure, GUSD holders could face losses proportional to their exposure to that protocol. The vault's multi-protocol allocation reduces concentration risk, but does not eliminate it entirely.</p><p><strong>Is GUSD audited?</strong></p><p>Audit status depends on the current deployment phase. Always check the official Status Network documentation or the vault's GitHub repository for up-to-date audit reports before depositing.</p><p><strong>How often does GUSD yield compound?</strong></p><p>Yield compounds continuously as underlying lending protocols generate interest. GUSD holders do not need to take any action, the redemption value increases automatically over time.</p><p><strong>Can I earn Karma by holding GUSD?</strong></p><p>Yes. Bridging yield-bearing assets including GUSD to Status Network earns Karma, the network's non-transferable reputation token. Karma determines your gasless transaction tier and grants governance voting power.</p><p><strong>What stablecoins can I deposit?</strong></p><p>USDC, USDT, and USDS are supported at launch. Additional stablecoins may be added as the vault expands.</p><p><strong>Are there deposit limits?</strong></p><p>Deposit limits depend on vault configuration and may change over time.
