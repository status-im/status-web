21:T272c,<p><strong>Impermanent loss</strong> is the difference in value between holding tokens in a liquidity pool and simply holding them in a wallet. It occurs when the price ratio of paired tokens changes after deposit. The loss is "impermanent" because it reverses if prices return to their original ratio.</p><p>But if an LP withdraws while prices have diverged, the loss becomes permanent.</p><h2 id="why-does-impermanent-loss-happen">Why Does Impermanent Loss Happen?</h2><p>Automated market makers (AMMs) use a formula to keep pools balanced. The most common version is the constant product formula: x * y = k.</p><p>When one token's price rises on external markets, arbitrage traders rebalance the pool. They buy the cheaper token from the pool and sell the expensive one into it.</p><p>This rebalancing changes the LP's token mix. The pool ends up holding more of the cheaper token and less of the expensive one.</p><p>The LP's total value falls behind what a simple "hold" strategy would have returned. That gap is impermanent loss.</p><h2 id="a-simple-example">A Simple Example</h2><p>Suppose you deposit 1 ETH and 2,000 USDC into a pool. ETH is priced at $2,000. Your total deposit is worth $4,000.</p><p>Now ETH doubles to $4,000. Arbitrage traders rebalance the pool.</p><p>Your position now holds roughly 0.707 ETH and 2,828 USDC. Total value: about $5,656.</p><p>If you had just held your original tokens, you would have 1 ETH ($4,000) + 2,000 USDC. Total: $6,000.</p><p>The difference ($344) is your impermanent loss. That is roughly 5.7% of the hold value.</p><h2 id="impermanent-loss-at-different-price-changes">Impermanent Loss at Different Price Changes</h2><p>This table shows impermanent loss for a standard 50/50 constant product pool.</p>

<p>The loss grows with price divergence. ETH/altcoin pools carry high risk.</p><p><strong>One-sided price surges.</strong> If one token moons while the other stays flat, the LP misses most of the upside.</p><p><strong>Short holding periods.</strong> Trading fees need time to accumulate. Exiting early means fees have not offset the loss yet.</p><h2 id="when-is-impermanent-loss-minimal">When Is Impermanent Loss Minimal?</h2><p><strong>Stablecoin pairs.</strong> USDC/DAI pools have near-zero price divergence. Impermanent loss stays negligible.</p><p><strong>Correlated assets.</strong> Pairs like stETH/ETH track each other closely. The price ratio rarely shifts far.</p><p><strong>High-volume pools.</strong> More trades generate more fees. Fees can exceed impermanent loss over time, making the position net-positive.</p><h2 id="how-trading-fees-offset-the-loss">How Trading Fees Offset the Loss</h2><p>LPs earn a share of every swap that passes through the pool. These fees accumulate continuously.</p><p>In a healthy pool, fee income exceeds impermanent loss. The LP earns a positive net return.</p><p>The key variable is fee revenue versus price divergence. High volume and low volatility favor the LP. Low volume and high volatility work against them.</p><p>This is why fee structure matters. Every basis point of unnecessary cost reduces the LP's ability to stay profitable.</p><h2 id="how-gas-costs-compound-the-problem">How Gas Costs Compound the Problem</h2><p>On Ethereum mainnet, <a href="https://status.network/blog/gasless-l2-vs-paymasters">gas fees</a> eat into LP profits in two ways.</p><p>First, entering and exiting a <a href="https://status.network/blog/why-ethereum-gas-fees-spike-eip-1559-explained">pool costs gas</a>. A deposit plus approval can cost $20 to $50 in gas during busy periods.</p><p>Second, claiming and compounding rewards costs gas each time. Small positions lose a larger share of returns to these fixed costs.</p><p>This creates a minimum viable position size. Below a certain threshold, gas costs erase all fee income.</p><h2 id="how-gasless-execution-changes-the-math">How Gasless Execution Changes the Math</h2><p>On a gasless Layer 2, the fixed-cost problem disappears. Entering, exiting, and compounding carry no transaction fees for the user.</p><p><a href="https://status.network/"><strong>Status Network</strong></a> operates as a gasless Ethereum L2. If the price ratio shifts beyond your comfort level, consider exiting.</p><p><strong>Use concentrated liquidity carefully.</strong> Concentrated positions earn more fees per dollar but face amplified impermanent loss if the price leaves your range.</p><p><strong>Factor in all costs.</strong> On high-fee networks, include gas in your profit calculation. On gasless networks, this step becomes unnecessary.</p><p><strong>Earn additional rewards.</strong> Some ecosystems offer extra incentives on top of trading fees. The loss only reverses if the price ratio returns exactly to its starting point.</p><p>In practice, prices rarely return to the exact original ratio. Most LPs experience some permanent loss relative to holding.</p><p>The real question is total return. Fee income plus any additional incentives minus impermanent loss equals net LP return.
