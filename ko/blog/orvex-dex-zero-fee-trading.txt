27:T527a,<p><a href="https://orvex.fi/">Orvex</a> is the native <a href="https://status.network/blog/dex-vs-cex-which-is-better-for-trading-in-2026">decentralized exchange (DEX)</a> built on <a href="https://status.network/">Status Network</a>, a fully gasless Ethereum Layer 2 built on reputation, composable privacy, and network-level spam protection. Orvex enables users to trade yield-bearing assets with zero gas fees, powered by Status Network’s gasless infrastructure and <a href="https://docs.status.network/general-info/gasless-transactions">Rate Limiting Nullifiers (RLN)</a>.</p><p>Orvex is not a standalone application. It is integral to Status Network’s <a href="https://status.network/blog/status-network-economic-model-yield-funding-pool-and-zero-gas">economic model</a>: swap fees and yield generated by bridged liquidity flow directly into the <a href="https://docs.status.network/tokenomics/public-funding">Apps Funding Pool</a>, funding developers, liquidity providers, and network operations. Karma holders can vote to allocate funding pool resources to specific Orvex liquidity pairs, directing incentives to where the community sees most value.</p><h2 id="what-is-orvex">What Is Orvex?</h2><p>Orvex is the flagship <strong>DEX</strong> on Status Network, designed to operate differently from traditional decentralized exchanges. Rather than charging users gas fees per swap, Orvex executes trades gaslessly by leveraging Status Network’s native yield infrastructure and reputation-based access control through <a href="https://docs.status.network/tokenomics/karmic-tokenomics">Karma</a>, a <a href="https://status.network/blog/what-is-a-soulbound-token-non-transferable-reputation-explained">soulbound non-transferable reputation token</a>.</p><p>Liquidity providers on Orvex earn from three simultaneous income streams:</p><ul><li>Native staking or lending yield from the underlying bridged assets</li><li>Traditional DEX swap fees (LP commissions)</li><li>Additional Karma rewards from Status Network’s governance system</li></ul><h2 id="how-zero-fee-trading-works-on-orvex">How Zero-Fee Trading Works on Orvex</h2><h3 id="gasless-execution-via-rln">Gasless Execution via RLN</h3><p>Orvex trades are gasless because Status Network uses Rate Limiting Nullifiers (RLN) to prevent spam instead of gas fees. RLN is a zero-knowledge cryptographic protocol that:</p><ol><li>Assigns each user a fair share of free transactions based on their Karma balance</li><li>Proves transaction validity without revealing the user’s identity (privacy via cryptographic commitments)</li><li>Enforces rate limits using Sparse Merkle Trees (height 20, supporting up to 1 million accounts)</li><li>Uses Shamir Secret Sharing and ZKPs to ensure no single entity controls the denial-of-service threshold</li></ol><p>When a user submits a swap on Orvex, the transaction is routed through Status Network’s sequencer, which validates the RLN proof and includes the trade in the next batch. No gas payment is required.</p><h3 id="revenue-model">Revenue Model</h3><p>Orvex’s revenue model is part of Status Network’s broader yield-backed economic model. For a full explanation of how revenue sources, the Apps Funding Pool, and zero gas work together, see <a href="https://status.network/blog/status-network-economic-model-yield-funding-pool-and-zero-gas">Status Network Economic Model: Revenue Sources, Funding Pool, and Zero Gas</a>.</p><p>In the context of Orvex specifically:</p><ul><li><strong>Native DEX swap fees</strong>: a percentage of swap volume flows into the Apps Funding Pool and network operations.</li><li><strong>Karma-governed pair funding</strong>: Karma holders vote to direct funding pool allocations to specific liquidity pairs on Orvex, boosting incentives for high-priority pools.</li><li><strong>Premium gas option</strong>: users exceeding their gasless quota can pay a premium fee to execute additional transactions, split between Status Network and the RLN protocol.</li></ul><h3 id="karma-based-transaction-tiers">Karma-Based Transaction Tiers</h3><p>Access to Orvex’s zero-fee trading is tiered by Karma, a <a href="https://status.network/blog/what-is-a-soulbound-token-non-transferable-reputation-explained">non-transferable soulbound token</a> representing reputation and contribution to Status Network. Users earn Karma through:</p><ul><li><a href="https://docs.status.network/tokenomics/snt-staking">SNT staking</a> (up to 9x multiplier for 4-year lock)</li><li>Bridging yield-bearing assets (ETH)</li><li>Providing liquidity to Orvex pools</li><li>Building and using applications on Status Network</li><li>Paying premium gas fees</li><li>Direct donations to the public funding pool</li></ul><p>Higher Karma unlocks higher daily transaction quotas, discouraging spam while rewarding long-term commitment. Karma minting parameters are set at launch and may evolve as the network matures.</p><h2 id="orvex-features-and-design">Orvex Features and Design</h2><h3 id="yield-boosted-liquidity-pools">Yield-Boosted Liquidity Pools</h3><p>Orvex pools are curated, meaning Status Network governance selects high-quality pairs to ensure capital efficiency. Each pool is anchored in a yield-bearing asset. Liquidity providers earn three income streams simultaneously: native yield from the underlying asset, traditional LP swap fees, and Karma rewards voted on by governance.</p><p>Karma holders vote on which pools receive additional incentive allocations from the <strong>Apps Funding Pool</strong>. This means the community actively directs yield toward pairs with the most strategic value for the ecosystem.</p><h3 id="privacy-via-bermuda">Privacy via Bermuda</h3><p>Orvex integrates Bermuda, Status Network’s native composable privacy layer, enabling:</p><ul><li>Confidential balance inquiries, users see their holdings without broadcasting to the network</li><li>Stealth accounts, trades linked to multiple addresses for unlinkability</li><li>Private swap execution, transaction amounts and endpoints obscured</li></ul><p>Privacy is optional; traders can choose public execution. The option exists natively, making Orvex unique among DEXs in offering privacy-by-design rather than as a bolt-on.</p><h3 id="liquidity-incentive-governance">Liquidity Incentive Governance</h3><p>Karma holders vote on DEX gauge incentives, allocating emissions and additional rewards to specific Orvex pools. This means the community, not a core team, decides which pairs attract the most capital. Incentive allocation evolves as the ecosystem grows, creating a governance-driven market design.</p><h2 id="why-orvex-matters-in-the-status-network-ecosystem">Why Orvex Matters in the Status Network Ecosystem</h2><p>Orvex is not just a trading venue. It is a core component of Status Network’s economic engine:</p><ol><li><strong>Revenue generation</strong>: swap fees from Orvex flow directly into the Apps Funding Pool, providing continuous capital to builders and liquidity providers.</li><li><strong>Flywheel effect</strong>: as Orvex volume grows, more fees accumulate. Karma holders allocate this capital to new apps, which onboard more users, which deepen Orvex liquidity, which increases yield. The loop is self-reinforcing.</li><li><strong>User acquisition</strong>: yield-bearing pairs and zero gas attract traders and LPs who discover other native apps, FIRM (CDP stablecoin), Punk.fun (token launchpad), and Bermuda (privacy layer).</li><li><strong>Reputation signaling</strong>: providing liquidity to Orvex earns Karma, signalling commitment to the network. Apps and protocols actively incentivise high-Karma users, creating a quality-of-user feedback loop.</li></ol><h2 id="technical-stack-built-on-linea-zkevm">Technical Stack: Built on Linea zkEVM</h2><p>Orvex runs on Status Network, which is built on <a href="https://linea.build/">Linea</a>’s open-source ZK-EVM rollup stack:</p><ul><li><strong>Fast finality</strong>: trades settle in seconds, with cryptographic proofs finalized on Ethereum.</li><li><strong>Ethereum security</strong>: all Orvex transactions are ultimately secured by Ethereum consensus.</li><li><strong>Composability</strong>: Orvex interacts seamlessly with other Status Network apps and Linea protocols.</li></ul><h2 id="orvex-vs-traditional-dexs-key-differences">Orvex vs. Traditional DEXs: Key Differences</h2>
