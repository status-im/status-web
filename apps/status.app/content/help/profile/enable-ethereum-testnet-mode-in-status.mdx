The [Ethereum blockchain](../getting-started/about-the-ethereum-blockchain.mdx) has two networks: Mainnet and testnet. Ethereum Mainnet is the primary network where actual transactions occur with real crypto.

Developers use Ethereum testnet for testing, which doesn't involve real crypto. In testnet mode, they can experiment with features and functionalities without risking their crypto.

When you run Status, your Wallet and token actions occur in the Ethereum Mainnet by default. This means you handle real cryptocurrencies and perform transactions on the main Ethereum network.

<Admonition type="caution">
  Ethereum testnet mode is intended only for advanced users and developers.
</Admonition>

## What to expect

- Don't enable testnet on your main Status installation. Use another computer or virtual machine with a different Status profile.
- When you enable the Ethereum testnet, everything in the Status app runs on testnet, including the communities you create or are part of.
- Don't mix Ethereum testnet and Mainet under the same Status profile. Doing so can lead to unexpected issues with your profile and communities.

## Enable testnet mode

  <TabsList size={32}>
    <TabsTrigger value="Mobile">
      Mobile
    </TabsTrigger>

    <TabsTrigger value="Desktop">
      Desktop
    </TabsTrigger>

    <TabsTrigger value="Web" disabled>
      Web
    </TabsTrigger>

  <TabsContent value="Mobile">
    1. From the tab bar, tap <ContextTag icon={<MessengerIcon />}>Messages</ContextTag>.
    1. Tap your avatar and then tap <ContextTag icon={<SettingsIcon />}>Advanced</ContextTag>
    1. Turn on <ContextTag>Testnet mode</ContextTag>.
    1. Click <ContextTag>OK</ContextTag> to confirm your action.

    <Admonition type="info">
      Always be cautious when using the <ContextTag icon={<SettingsIcon />}>Advanced</ContextTag> options. Don't change these options unless you know what you're doing.
    </Admonition>
  </TabsContent>

  <TabsContent value="Desktop">
    1. From the navigation sidebar, click <ContextTag icon={<SettingsDesktopIcon />}>Settings</ContextTag> > <ContextTag icon={<WalletIcon />}>Wallet</ContextTag> > <ContextTag>Networks</ContextTag>.
    1. Find the <ContextTag>Advanced</ContextTag> section and enable <ContextTag>Testnet mode</ContextTag>.
    1. Click <ContextTag>Turn on testnet mode</ContextTag> to confirm your action.

    <Admonition type="info">
      Always be cautious when using the <ContextTag icon={<SettingsDesktopIcon />}>Advanced</ContextTag> options. Don't change these options unless you know what you're doing.
    </Admonition>
  </TabsContent>
</Tabs>

## Enable testnet for Status Communities

By default, you can't create or manage Status Communities on testnet. To enable this feature, follow these steps in addition to [enabling testnet mode](#enable-testnet-mode) in your Status app:

<Tabs defaultValue="Desktop">
  <TabsList size={32}>
    <TabsTrigger value="Mobile" disabled>
      Mobile
    </TabsTrigger>

    <TabsTrigger value="Desktop">
      Desktop
    </TabsTrigger>

    <TabsTrigger value="Web" disabled>
      Web
    </TabsTrigger>
  </TabsList>

  <TabsContent value="Desktop">
    1. From the navigation sidebar, click <ContextTag icon={<SettingsDesktopIcon />}>Settings</ContextTag> > <ContextTag icon={<SettingsDesktopIcon />}>Advanced</ContextTag>.
    1. Scroll down to the <ContextTag>Developer features</ContextTag> section.
    1. Find and enable <ContextTag>Manage communities on testnet</ContextTag>.
  </TabsContent>
</Tabs>

<Admonition type="info">
  This option is only available on Status desktop.
</Admonition>
