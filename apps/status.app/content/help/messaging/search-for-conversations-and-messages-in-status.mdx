<Admonition type="info">
  Currently, you can only search for conversations and messages in the Status desktop app.
</Admonition>

In the Status app, you can search for specific conversations ([direct messages](./send-direct-messages-to-your-contacts.mdx), [group chats](./create-a-group-chat.mdx) and [channels](../communities/channels-your-quick-start-guide.mdx)) as well as text within those conversations.

## What to Expect

- The [message search](#search-for-messages) function only includes messages in your chat window, including those you can scroll through. Older messages beyond this may not appear.
- You can search messages in [token-gated communities and channels](../communities/token-based-access-to-communities-and-channels.mdx) as long as you're a community member with access to the channel.
- On Status desktop, you can use keyboard shortcuts or the <ContextTag icon={<SearchDesktopIcon />}>Search</ContextTag> option in the user interface.

## Search for conversations

You can search for conversations in direct messages, group chats and channels you are part of.

  <TabsContent value="Desktop">

    1. Enter the name of the direct message, group chat or channel you want to search for.
    1. From the results list, click the direct message, group chat or channel you want to open.
    1. ## Search for messages

You can search for messages by entering text you remember from the conversation.

<Tabs defaultValue="Desktop">
  <TabsList size={32}>
    <TabsTrigger value="Mobile">
      Mobile
    </TabsTrigger>

    <TabsTrigger value="Desktop">
      Desktop
    </TabsTrigger>

    <TabsTrigger value="Web" disabled>
      Web
    </TabsTrigger>
  </TabsList>

  <TabsContent value="Mobile">
    <Admonition type="info">
      Currently, you can only search for messages in the Status desktop app.
    </Admonition>
  </TabsContent>

  <TabsContent value="Desktop">
    1. From the conversation you want to search, press <ContextTag icon={<KeyboardDesktopIcon />}>Ctrl + F</ContextTag> (Windows or Linux) or <ContextTag icon={<KeyboardDesktopIcon />}>Cmd + F</ContextTag> (macOS) to open the search bar.
    1. Enter the text you want to search for.
    1. Alternatively, you can click <ContextTag icon={<SearchDesktopIcon />}>Search</ContextTag> at the top of the content area to open the search bar.

    <Admonition type="tip">
      From the search bar, click the <ContextTag icon={<DropdownIcon />}>Drop down</ContextTag> to choose another conversation, or select <ContextTag>Anywhere</ContextTag> to search all conversations.
    </Admonition>
  </TabsContent>
</Tabs>
