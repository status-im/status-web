language: en
title: Possessive form
---

- Use the possessive form with caution and avoid complex possessive forms like the [compound possessive](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/possessive-case/). Some non-native English readers may have problems understanding sentences with possessive forms.

<Table>
  <TableHead>
    <TableHeader>Usage</TableHeader>
    <TableHeader>Example</TableHeader>
  </TableHead>
  <TableContent>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>[...] your contact's status.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Incorrect</TableCell>
      <TableCell>your contact's verified status.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>[...] the verified status of the contact.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>[...] the picture shared by you and your trusted contact.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Incorrect</TableCell>
      <TableCell>[...] you and your trusted contact's shared picture.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </TableContent>
</Table>

- Use a single straight quotation mark (') apostrophe. Don't use curly quotation marks (also known as smart quotes.)
- If you have to use a passive form with a proper name ending with the possessive *s*, add the apostrophe at the end without an extra *s* letter.


<Table>
  <TableHead>
    <TableHeader>Usage</TableHeader>
    <TableHeader>Example</TableHeader>
  </TableHead>
  <TableContent>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Check your contacts' list.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Incorrect</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Check your contacts's list.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </TableContent>
</Table>

- If the possessor is not a person, you don't need to add an apostrophe to show possession.
