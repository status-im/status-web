title: Sentences and paragraphs
---

- Write sentences of a maximum of 25 words and paragraphs of no more than 50-60 words. Rewrite the content and break sentences and paragraphs as necessary.
- Include *the* and *that* to clarify your sentence.

    </TableRow>
  </TableContent>
</Table>

- Don't use sentence connectors: *So*, *Thus*, *Therefore*, *Consequently*, *As a consequence*, *For this reason*, *Hence*, and so on.

<Table>
  <TableHead>
    <TableHeader>Usage</TableHeader>
    <TableHeader>Example</TableHeader>
  </TableHead>
  <TableContent>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Your seed phrase provides access to your crypto assets. Keep your seed phrase safe.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Incorrect</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Your seed phrase provides access to your crypto assets. Therefore, keep your seed phrase safe.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </TableContent>
</Table>

- Avoid long [noun phrases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noun_phrase). Don't use more than one modifier if you use a noun phrase. Use the *of* preposition to simplify noun phrases.

  <TableHead>

  </TableHead>
  <TableContent>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>The default lifetime of a token value is one year.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Incorrect</TableCell>
      <TableCell>The default token value lifetime is one year.</TableCell>

- Keep adjectives and adverbs immediately before the word they're modifying. Pay close attention to *only* and *not*.

<Table>
  <TableHead>
    <TableHeader>Usage</TableHeader>
    <TableHeader>Example</TableHeader>
  </TableHead>
  <TableContent>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>**Correct**</TableCell>
      <TableCell>You can access your messages only after 30 days.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Incorrect</TableCell>
      <TableCell>You can only access your messages after 30 days.</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </TableContent>
</Table>

- In the first paragraph after a heading, avoid repeating the title information. For example, if the heading title is "Search for a contact using the Status display name," don't start the first paragraph with "To search for a contact using the Status display name [...]"
- Lookup for instances of the *which* word on your text; it's possible you can replace them with *that*. For more information about the differences between *which* and *that*, check out this [Grammarly article](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/which-vs-that/).
